Compilation and Translation Review online

The Compilation and Translation Review, founded in September 2008, is a semi-annual academic journal. It serves as a platform for experts and scholars to publish and exchange research findings in compilation and translation, aiming to promote the development of these studies in Taiwan. The journal also seeks to enhance the effectiveness of the National Academy for Educational Research* in compiling and translating academic works and in reviewing terminology. As a publication that integrates both theory and practice, it welcomes a variety of submissions. In addition to academic papers on compilation, translation studies, and related industry developments, it also features specialized articles such as forums, book reviews, translation critiques, and annotated translations. With contributors from around the world, it stands as one of the few professional academic journals in Taiwan dedicated to the field of compilation and translation.
